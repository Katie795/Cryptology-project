The Pigpen Cipher

History

This is a classic cipher which has been popular throughout history, though its origins are unknown. It known to have been used by the Church, and more notoriously, by the Freemasons, which is why is is sometimes referred to as the "Freemasons Cipher". It can be found enscribed on some of their gravestones and in old documents.

How it works

This cipher is constructed from grids, as you can see. Each letter of the alphabet corresponds with one open space in the grid, and this lets it fall into the category of a Simple Substitution Cipher. Therefore looking at the grids, it is fairly straightforward to encode and decode a message relatively quickly!

How to solve

If the grid system was unknown to you, a message written in the Pigpen system would look pretty confusing! How would you go about cracking the code? It turns out that the Pigpen cipher is not very secure against a clever codebreaker. If you were lucky, you could figure out what the system was using a bit of intuition and guesswork. Nowadays, this type of cipher is so well known that, armed with the knowledge you now have, anything written in this code is suceptible to attack!
