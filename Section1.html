The Monoalphabetic Substitution Cipher

This is the name given to any substitution cipher where the cipher alphabet is either letters or symbols. A Substitution cipher. There are 40000000000000000000000000 possible outcomes. Frequency analysis invented by Al Kindi, the ‘Philosopher of Arabs’ Short texts likely to deviate from the norm. Until 1500 the codebreakers had the upper hand, was the safest form of encryption. Describe how to crack. Describe and example The key to cracking this cipher lies in the fact that some letters appear more than others, depending on the language. For example, Al Kindi found that the letters a and l appear more often that the other letters in Arabic. Likewise, in English, the most common letter in English is e, which occurs 12.7% of the time, followed by t, then a, then o. Of course, the shorter the text, the more likely the letters are to deviate from the norm! Example Challenge
